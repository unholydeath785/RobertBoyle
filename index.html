Early Life

Robert Boyle was born in Stalbridge, Ireland in 1627. He was born into one of the wealthiest families in Britain due to his father being the Lord Treasurer of the Country. Boyle grew up under this roof as a son of an aristocrat and these views stuck with him his whole life. Not much occurred during Boyle’s youth due to his wealthy upbringing. During the end of Roberts youth, he was accepted into Eton college where he studied, although this was split part time with studying from home. In order to finish off his education he went to explore the rest of Europe and spent a few months in Italy, France, and Switzerland. Traveling around Europe affected Boyle’s view of the world and formed his desire to explain science and how it relates to the religion which shaped his life. Due to Boyle’s deep theism his studies turned away from writing to focus upon natural philosophy.

Robert Boyle Timeline

1627 Birth

25 January, Robert Boyle is born in Lismore, Ireland. Boyle was bithed as the seventh son of Richard Boyle, 1st Earl of Cork, by his second wife, Catherine.

1635 College

Robert Boyle enters Eton College with his brother, Francis Boyle, on October 2nd.

1638 Leaves College

On November 23rd Robert Boyle leaves Eton college.

1639 Traveling Europe

Robert travels to France and Switzerland with his brother Francis, under the tutelage of Isaac Marcombes. Robert then spends several months in Geneva, Switzerland.

1641 Italy

Robert travels to Italy to continue his exploration of Europe and the world around him.

1642 France

Robert is stuck in Marseiles, France and decides to travel back to Geneva, where he earlier spent 2 years.

1644 England

Robert returns to England after spending another 2 years in Switerland.

1645 Stalbridge

Settles at Stalbridge, Dorset, where he spends much of the next decade.

1649 Labratory

Robert sets up his laboratory in Stalbridge.

1652 Visits Ireland

Robert goes back to Ireland to see his homeland.

1654 Illness

While visiting his homeland of Ireland, Robert Boyle has a severe illness that affects his eyesight.

1655 Oxford

Robert moves from Stalbridge to Oxford to enjoy the flow of knowledge and continue his scientific works.

1659 Air-pump

In Oxford Robert Boyle's scientific work with his partner, Robert Hooke, first execute experiments with an air-pump. During these expirements he discovered a law which is P • V = k. This law states that pressure multiplied by volume is equal to a constant mass.

1660 Royal Society

28 November Robert attends inaugural meeting of Royal Society, which Robert was one of the founders of.

1662 Governor

Robert is appointed first Governor of the Corporation for Propagation of the Gospel in New England.

1668 London

Robert settles in London, which is his final residence. He lives in London for the rest of his life with Lady Ranelagh.

1670 Illness

In 1670 Robert again suffers another severe medical condition, a stroke, which leaves him in a tough state.

1680 Royal Society

On the 18th of Decemeber Robert Boyle is offered the presidency of the Royal Society. RObert declined the offer.

1689 Governor

On August 22nd, 1689 Robert Boyle stepped down as the acting governor for the Corporation for Propagation of the Gospel in New England.

1691

On the 18th of July Robert signed his final will and testiment in his London home. Later in the year on the 22nd of December Lady Ranelagh dies, leaving Robert alone for another 8 days before he himself passes away on the 31st of December.

1692 Funeral

On the turn of the new year, Robert Boyle is laid to rest at his funeral on January 7th, 1692.
